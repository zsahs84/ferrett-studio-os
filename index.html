🔌 Cable & Connector Cheat-Sheet

TS — ¼" Mono
Unbalanced. Tip + Sleeve. Instruments & short unbalanced runs. On the Volt: back main outs & the instrument input.
TRS — ¼" Balanced
Tip + Ring + Sleeve. Balanced mono (long runs, hum-free) or stereo on headphones. Not interchangeable with TS in balanced gear.
XLR — Mic
Balanced, locking. Carries +48 V phantom for condensers. Volt: INST switch OFF for mic/line.
RCA — Consumer
Unbalanced, ~-10 dBV. Hi-fi gear (your Sansui Tape 1). ¼" TS → RCA adapters take the Volt out to the amp.
Rules of thumb
  • Phantom: only for condensers/active DI. Turn it OFF for ribbons & guitars. Mute before toggling.
  • INST switch: ON = Hi-Z ¼" instrument · OFF = line/mic level.
  • Levels: +4 dBu (pro/balanced) is ~12 dB hotter than -10 dBV (consumer/RCA) — expect a jump.
  • Hum: prefer balanced (XLR/TRS) for anything over a few feet; keep audio away from power bricks.
